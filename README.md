# FundNProof

**Trust infrastructure for public money in Indonesia.**

Every year, billions in donations, social funds, and community budgets flow through systems that people cannot independently verify.

People keep asking the same question:

**"Where did my money actually go?"**

FundNProof answers that question with verifiable proof, not promises.

---

## The Problem

Trust in public fund management is fragile.

* Donors cannot clearly see how their money is used
* Organizations rely on static reports that are difficult to verify
* Communities depend on trust instead of proof
* Data can be modified without detection

Today, transparency is claimed, not proven.

This leads to:

* Low trust from donors
* Reduced funding potential
* Friction in fundraising and reporting

---

## The Solution

FundNProof turns financial transparency into something verifiable.

It combines:

* Financial management (ERP for funds, donors, programs)
* Cryptographic integrity (hash-linked records)
* Blockchain anchoring on Solana

Every transaction becomes:

* Recorded
* Tamper-evident
* Publicly verifiable

Instead of asking *"Where did my money go?"*, users can verify it directly.

---

## First Use Case: Donation Transparency

The system starts with donation flows, where trust is most critical.

When a donation is made:

1. The transaction is recorded in the system
2. It is hashed and linked to previous records
3. A batch of transactions is anchored on-chain
4. Anyone can verify the data independently

Donors can confirm:

* Their transaction exists
* The data has not been altered
* It is part of a verifiable ledger

---

## How It Works

* Transactions are stored in a structured financial system
* Each record is hashed and linked to form a tamper-evident chain
* Periodically, a Merkle root is anchored to Solana
* Verification can be done through the app or directly on-chain

This creates two layers:

* Off-chain system for usability
* On-chain proof for trust

## Why Indonesia

Indonesia has strong donation and community funding culture, including charity, crowdfunding, and social programs.

At the same time:

* Trust issues are common
* Verification tools are limited
* Most systems still rely on manual reporting

FundNProof introduces verifiable transparency to an environment where it is urgently needed.

## Key Considerations

Adoption  
Users are not familiar with cryptographic verification. The system must remain simple and accessible.

Trust  
Although the system is trustless by design, initial user trust must still be built through clarity and usability.

Data Integrity  
Blockchain ensures data cannot be changed, but does not guarantee correctness at input. Additional validation layers are required.

Cost  
Target users may have limited budgets. Pricing strategy must balance accessibility and sustainability.

Regulation  
Handling financial data requires awareness of compliance and local regulations.
